The world malaria report of 2018, indicates that 11 million cases of malaria were recorded in the country.
In addition to that, malaria is still the leading cause of death among Ugandans; these account for 19% of the people who die annually.
The Ministry of Health says, Malaria is still a public health problem, affecting mostly children below five years and pregnant women.
The good news is that; Uganda has made efforts to fight malaria.
Proper nutrition is important for people with malaria because it helps in reducing the complications and is key in the treatment of malaria.
The body's ability to fight any disease depends on one's nutrition.
Malaria patients are advised to eat foods that boost their blood levels like liver, meat, green vegetables, carbohydrates and plenty of fruits.
They also need to take a lot of water due to the increased body temperature.

Kiwanuuzibwa nti Ssekabaka Nnakibinge bwe yali atabaala Abannyolo, yalagiriza mutabani w'oMussesse Wannema eyali ayitibwa Kyobe amuyambeko.
